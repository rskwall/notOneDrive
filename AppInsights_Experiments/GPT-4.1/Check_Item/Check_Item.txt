# LegaSuite DEMO Application Documentation

## Overview

The LegaSuite DEMO application is an interactive IBM i (AS/400) demonstration tool designed to showcase the capabilities of LegaSuite products. It provides a menu-driven interface for exploring various business functions, such as customer and item maintenance, order entry, and reporting. This application is for demonstration purposes only and is not intended for real-time business use.

---

## Main Menu Navigation

Upon logging in, you are presented with the **LegaSuite DEMO Main Menu**. The menu options are selected by entering the corresponding number at the `===>` prompt and pressing **Enter**.

### Main Menu Options

| Option | Description                    |
|--------|--------------------------------|
| 1      | Maintain Customers             |
| 2      | Customer Payments              |
| 3      | Maintain Items                 |
| 4      | Incoming Stock                 |
| 6      | Order Entry                    |
| 7      | Delivered Orders               |
| 9      | View Customer Turn over        |
| 10     | View Item Turn over            |
| 80     | End of year procedure          |
| 90     | Sign off                       |

---

## Example Navigation Steps

### 1. Accessing Item Maintenance

- At the main menu, type `3` at the `===>` prompt and press **Enter**.
- The **Maintain Items** screen appears.
- To search for an item, press **F4** (Prompt) or **F14** (Search description).
- To add a new item, press **F6** (Add).
- To exit, press **F3** (Exit) or **F12** (Cancel).

### 2. Selecting and Editing an Item

- On the item list, select an item by entering its number in the `Item Number` field and press **Enter**.
- The item details screen will display:
  - Item Number
  - Description
  - Price
  - Stock quantity
  - Turnover for the year
- To update the price or other fields, enter the new value and press **Enter**.
- To delete the item, press **F23** (Delete).
- To return to the previous menu, press **F3** (Exit) or **F12** (Cancel).

### 3. Signing Off

- At any menu, type `90` at the `===>` prompt and press **Enter** to sign off.
